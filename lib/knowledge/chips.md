# FPL Chip Strategy — expert principles

Use these principles to reason about chip *timing and sequencing*. The manager's actual
chip availability, gameweek, and recommended windows are supplied separately as facts — apply
the principles to those facts. Prefer "roughly right" over "perfect": the gap between the best
and second-best chip plan is small (~3 pts), but optimal vs. random is 20–30+ pts a season, so
the priority is to use chips on a sensible Double/Blank rather than to nail the exact gameweek.

## Season-specific rules (2025/26 — update each season)
- **Two sets of chips.** One Wildcard, Free Hit, Bench Boost, and Triple Captain in *each half*
  of the season — **8 chips total**.
- **First-half set expires at the GW19 deadline (30 Dec 2025)** and does NOT carry over. Treat
  unused first-half chips as **use-it-or-lose-it** as GW19 approaches.
- **Second-half set unlocks at GW20.** **One chip per gameweek** — never two in the same week.
- No Assistant Manager chip this season.

## Canonical timing (applies within each half)
- **Wildcard** — play it *before the biggest fixture swing*, when several of your players hit a
  bad run at once, or to set up a Double Gameweek squad. It restructures the team; don't waste it
  on one or two transfers you could do normally.
- **Bench Boost** — play it on a **Double Gameweek** when all 15 players have strong fixtures,
  ideally the GW *after* a wildcard rebuild so the bench is loaded with double-fixture starters.
- **Free Hit** — play it on a **Blank Gameweek** (field a full XI when the template blanks), or on
  a big Double to attack a one-week ceiling. It reverts after one week, so it never damages
  long-term structure — the "get out of jail" card.
- **Triple Captain** — play it on a **premium's Double Gameweek** (two good fixtures, nailed to
  start, high ceiling). A single great home fixture can justify it, but a DGW is the textbook spot.

## Sequencing & judgment
- **Plan the half as a sequence**, not four isolated picks: e.g., Wildcard into a Double → Bench
  Boost the next Double → Free Hit the Blank. The fixture calendar (which GWs are Doubles/Blanks)
  drives the order more than any single-week return.
- Doubles and Blanks are only *known* once the Premier League reschedules fixtures (usually the big
  ones land GW18+ and GW28+). Before they're confirmed, **hold chips** and give general guidance
  rather than committing to an exact gameweek.
- **Hits around chips** can be worth it: taking a −4 to set up a Bench-Boost/Triple-Captain Double,
  or stacking transfers onto a Free Hit week, often pays back — judge it on the projected uplift vs.
  the 4-point cost.
- **Don't hoard.** A chip unused by its deadline is a wasted ~10–15 points; a slightly-suboptimal
  play beats no play. As a half's deadline nears, bias toward using a chip on the best available
  Double/Blank rather than waiting for a perfect week that may not come.
