# FPL Rank Strategy — effective ownership & chase-vs-protect

Use these principles to reason about *rank*, not just raw projected points. The manager's
actual rank, trend, gameweeks remaining, and each candidate's ownership are supplied as facts —
apply the principles to them. The core idea: your rank moves relative to everyone else, so what
matters is your points *vs the field*, which ownership governs.

## Effective ownership (EO)
- EO is how much of the rank pool effectively owns a player, **captaincy-weighted** (a captained
  player counts roughly double). It's the single most important lever for rank.
- **Captaining a high-EO player protects rank** — you move with the crowd; if he hauls you don't
  lose ground, if he blanks neither do your rivals.
- **A low-EO (differential) captain is a rank gamble** — you gain ground if it hits and the
  template blanks, but bleed rank if it misses. Captaincy is where EO bets pay off or hurt most.

## The template
- The "template" is the set of highly-owned core players. **Covering it limits downside**: when a
  template player hauls and you don't own him, you bleed rank across the entire field at once.
- Owning the template is the safe baseline; differentials are deliberate, situational deviations
  from it — not a default.

## Chase vs protect (continuous — scale it, don't switch it)
- **Protect** (strong/rising rank, a healthy lead, or early season): own and captain the template,
  avoid needless differentials. Variance is your enemy when you're ahead.
- **Chase** (falling rank, a large gap to your target, few gameweeks left): take differentials —
  matching the template only **locks in** your deficit; you need variance to close a gap.
- Scale aggression with **gap size × time pressure**: a bigger gap and fewer gameweeks justify
  bolder, lower-ownership picks; a small gap or lots of time left calls for patience.

## Judgment
- A **differential** (roughly sub-10% owned) is worth it when its projected upside justifies the
  ownership risk *given the manager's rank situation* — never as a reflex.
- **Don't over-differentiate from a winning position**: chasing variance when you're ahead is how
  good ranks get thrown away.
- When advising, make the rank logic explicit: *why* template vs differential here, given this
  manager's gap and time left — not just which player.
